# Data Science and Machine Learning Assignment

## 1. Definition of Data Science and Machine Learning

### Data Science
Data Science is the process of collecting, cleaning, analyzing, and interpreting data to generate useful insights for decision-making. For example, a business owner may record every product sold in a store to identify the most popular products, peak customer hours, and the most profitable items.

### Machine Learning
Machine Learning (ML) is a subfield of Data Science that enables computers to learn patterns from data and make predictions without being explicitly programmed.

---

## 3. Supervised Learning vs Unsupervised Learning

| Supervised Learning | Unsupervised Learning |
|--------------------|----------------------|
| Uses labeled data | Uses unlabeled data |
| Predicts known outcomes | Discovers hidden patterns |
| Example: Spam Detection | Example: Customer Segmentation |

---

## 4. Overfitting

Overfitting occurs when a Machine Learning model memorizes training data instead of learning general patterns. As a result, the model performs well on training data but poorly on unseen data.

### Methods to Reduce Overfitting
- Use more training data
- Cross-validation
- Simpler models
- Regularization techniques

---

## 5. Training Data and Test Data

Training data is used to teach a model, while test data is used to evaluate its performance on unseen examples.

Example:

- Training Set: 80%
- Test Set: 20%

---

## 6. Case Study: IBM Watson Health

IBM Watson Health uses Data Science and Machine Learning to assist healthcare professionals in diagnosis and treatment planning.

### Key Contributions

- Improved cancer diagnosis accuracy by approximately 15%.
- Reduced treatment planning time from weeks to a few days.
- Reduced medication errors by 30%.
- Processed over 200 million pages of medical literature.

### Role of Data Science

Data Science helps collect, clean, integrate, and analyze patient records, genomic information, and medical literature.

### Role of Machine Learning

Machine Learning models identify patterns in healthcare data and provide personalized diagnostic and treatment recommendations.
